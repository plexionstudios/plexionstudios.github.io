The History of My Channel

I mean, not all of it, but most of it.

Where it all started.

My first video back in 2018 was this one, I simply taught people how to do something in Scratch. If i'm being honest, the only things bad about this video are the lack of any audio and the text that moves at 20000mph. Now, I don't know how this happened, but it did become my most viewed video for quite some time!

And it continued..

Seeing that people liked the first video I thought I would make another one. The first idea that came to my mind was quite a strange one "Let's teach people how to draw a Diamond Sword!". To this day, I don't know how it was a tutorial, teaching the viewer absolutely nothing.. It was my first video with a microphone though! I ended up editing the video and re-uploading it for Plexmas.

200 VIEWS!

In April 2019 (not april fools), I published a video called 'PewDiePie Congratulations - 8 Bit Mix' featuring a mix of an 8-bit congratulations remix and the official vocals. The video became by 2nd most viewed video with 288 views total, until recently when it was overtaken by a Karlson video.

Plexmas 2019.

Walking to school one day I had another one of my ideas, "How about I make a special series for Christmas this year, it won't be that hard to schedule". I then made full branding and ran it from the 13th of December up until the 17th where it was unfortunately cancelled due to a lack of exported videos. However, just because it failed this year, doesn't mean it can't happen again! ;)

PogChamp.

After school on a Friday, I recorded a video on a new game made by a YouTuber I watch, DaniDev! I downloaded the game and recorded myself playing it. Not expecting much attention the video, I went and uploaded it and went to sleep. I woke up to a phone flooded of notifications from different comments, likes and subscriptions; it was surreal! Once again, thanks everyone who subscribed then and stuck around, it's greatly appreciated!

The Aftermath.

I appreciate everyone who watches my videos and subscribes, it means so much! Now I create videos and streams on varied games.
